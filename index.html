About

Profile

My work sits at the intersection of language technology and humanistic inquiry, with projects ranging from topic segmentation and audio representations to language models for moral values, historical language processing, and efficient summarization.

Research direction

I work on models that help analyse long and structured sequences, compare sentence encoders, and bridge text with audio and multimodal content.

I am especially interested in methods that remain useful outside ideal benchmark settings and in interdisciplinary applications that connect AI with cultural and social data.

Personal note
  • I play tennis.
  • I enjoy explaining complex AI concepts to students from technical and non-technical backgrounds.
  • I value mentorship as part of research and teaching practice.
